Caesar Cipher Challenge

A Caesar cipher is a simple and ancient encryption technique where each letter in the plaintext is shifted a certain number of places down or up the alphabet. It's named after Julius Caesar, who reportedly used this method to encrypt his correspondence.

How it Works:

  1. For example, with a key of 3:
    • A becomes D
    • B becomes E
    • C becomes F
    • and so on...
  2. Wrap around at the end of the alphabet, so:
    • X becomes A
    • Y becomes B
    • Z becomes C

To decrypt a message encrypted with a Caesar cipher, you simply shift the letters back by the same number of places.
