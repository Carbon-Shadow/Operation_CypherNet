Pigpen Cipher

The pigpen cipher is a basic monoalphabetic substitution cipher that dates back to ancient times. It has many names: tic-tac-toe, Rosicrucian, Napoleon, Freemason, and masonic cipher. It uses a divided geometry of two grid types and some strategically placed dots that map to a plaintext letter.

How Pigpen Cipher is Used:

  • Historical Context:
    • Used by various groups such as the Freemasons in the 18th century.
    • Examples include encrypting simple messages with easily recognizable symbols.
  • Modern Usage:
    • Found in puzzles, games, and educational settings to demonstrate basic encryption techniques.
    • Popular among hobbyists and those interested in historical ciphers.

Pigpen cipher provides a simple method of encoding and decoding messages using a substitution system based on geometric symbols.
